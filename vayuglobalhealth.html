Oxygen Concentrator Research Project

  • Role: Lead Engineer
  • Summary: Vayu had developed a revolutionary oxygen blender that diluted the oxygen provided to newborns on a bCPAP. However, the blender was only functional with oxygen tanks due to their high pressure. This is suitable in emergencies, however, most patients need to be on a bCPAP for several days and that requires several oxygen tanks. An oxygen concentrator, on the other hand, can deliver oxygen consistently as long as it is plugged in but it operates at much lower pressure. The goal then was to see if the blender geometry could be adapted to function under lower pressure.
  • Scope: Testing with a World Health Organization recommended oxygen concentrator and 3D print different geometry styles to determine if a new design can achieve the same oxygen concentration range as the original blender but at a lower supply pressure.
  • Process: The first part of the project involved researching and purchasing an oxygen concentrator on the recommended WHO list. Then, I developed a testing plan in which I could complete the research by the end of my six-month fellowship. After defining the necessary technical specifications, I started designing and 3D printing several prototypes for testing.
  • Skills: 3D printing, rapid-prototyping, testing plan
  • Results: At the end of my fellowship I had tested over 20 different oxygen blenders. I was able to develop a new geometry which could reach lower oxygen concentrations than the original when powered with an oxygen concentrator, however, I determined it was not possible to make one that could reach the full oxygen concentration range.
