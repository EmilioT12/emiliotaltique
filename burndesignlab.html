Burn Design Lab

An organization focused on the development of clean cookstoves for low-income settings

New Stove Model Development

  • Role: Lead Engineer
  • Summary: A client in Mozambique wanted to develop a stove for the low-income population that could be manufactured locally with limited tooling.
  • Scope: Develop three prototypes from which the client would select one to move into production. The stoves needed to meet the criteria for cost, manufacturability with limited tooling, and efficiency. There was also a Human-Centered Design approach in which other preferences from the local population would be taken from surveys. Once a design was selected, we would help in building up a factory to produce the stove.
  • Process: I started the first prototype by going off "standard stove" dimensions because we had no data from Mozambique at the time to steer us toward any particular size or shape, and once we did we could alter it to their needs. The biggest hurdle to developing a working prototype was the clay liner where the charcoal would go. Burn Design Lab had no previous knowledge of this, so I was tasked with learning about ceramics to the point where we could develop them in our lab for this project and future ones. In a month I learned as much as I could about ceramics and was able to mold and fire several different "liners" for use in prototypes. From there, I was able to build a first prototype in about two weeks using sheet metal, cement, a hand-molded clay liner, and gravel filler.
  • Skills: 3D CAD, rapid-prototyping, testing plan
  • Results: Unfortunately, I was only able to get as far as doing some initial tests on the prototype before I had to leave the position due to family health issues.

Stove Factory Improvement Project

  • Role: Lead Engineer
  • Summary: A client in Uganda operated a stove factory in which they wanted to triple production capacity from 5,000 stoves per month to 15,000.
  • Scope: The client wanted us to focus on identifying necessary equipment, training Lean methodology, improving current manufacturing methods, identifying new suppliers, and overseeing new building construction.
  • Process: This was a multi-year project in which I traveled back and forth to the factory for several weeks, including serving as the interim factory manager for a three-month stint. The biggest bottleneck for the entire factory was determined to be processing sheet metal for the body of the stove. The solution was twofold: first, to implement several simple hand-held tools for processes like hems and complicated cuts. The second was to customize hand-powered sheet metal table and circle shears. This was necessary because the factory had unreliable electricity and there is no existing product for the stove's extremely thin sheet metal.
  • Skills: Lean Methodology, Project management, Supply chain management
  • Results: The new shears were successfully implemented and some of the new buildings were finished, which brought the production capacity to 12,000 units, however, the client is still working on securing enough funding to purchase the remaining equipment to complete the project.

Table Shear Customization

  • Role: Project Engineer
  • Summary: As referenced in the factory improvement project, the customization and implementation of a manual table shear was crucial to increasing the production capacity of the stove factory.
  • Scope: The goal was to customize an existing manual table shear so that it could accommodate thin sheet metal and set to cut depths from 10 to 950 mm. The customization also needed to be low-cost and able to be broken down so it could fly as luggage.
  • Process: On top of building initial concepts and assisting with the design, my main responsibility was to assemble the machine in Uganda and train the staff on how to use and maintain it.
  • Skills: FEA analysis, Solidworks, Machining
  • Results: The machine was successfully implemented and within a day it was being used. Watching the staff use equipment that I spent a year designing and building was truly one of the highlights of my career so far.
